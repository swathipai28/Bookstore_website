The Valley of Fear

Author:Sherlock Holmes

Author Desription:Sherlock Holmes is a fictional detective created by British author Arthur Conan Doyle. Referring to himself as a "consulting detective" in his stories, Holmes is known for his proficiency with observation, deduction, forensic science and logical reasoning that borders on the fantastic, which he employs when investigating cases for a wide variety of clients, including Scotland Yard.

Price: $20

Description:

"The Valley of Fear" is divided into two distinct parts. The first part revolves around the enigmatic murder of Mr. John Douglas, a wealthy resident of Birlstone Manor. Sherlock Holmes is called upon to unravel the perplexing circumstances surrounding the crime. As the investigation progresses, Holmes and Watson uncover a web of deceit, secret codes, and a mysterious organization known as the Scowrers. The second part of the novel takes the reader back in time to the United States, delving into the origins of the crime and shedding light on the sinister activities of the Scowrers. The narrative presents a gripping account of betrayal, revenge, and the far-reaching consequences of past actions.
